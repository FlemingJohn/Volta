# IEC 60502-1: Power Cables with Extruded Insulation (1 kV - 3 kV)

## 1. Scope and General
**IEC 60502 Part 1** specifies the construction, dimensions, and test requirements for power cables with extruded solid insulation for rated voltages of:
* **1 kV** ($U_m = 1.2 \text{ kV}$)
* **3 kV** ($U_m = 3.6 \text{ kV}$)

It covers cables for fixed installations such as distribution networks or industrial installations.

### 1.1 Voltage Designation
Cables are designated by the voltages $U_0/U (U_m)$:
* **$U_0$**: Rated voltage between conductor and earth.
* **$U$**: Rated voltage between conductors.
* **$U_m$**: Maximum system voltage.
    * *Standard Rating:* $0.6/1 \text{ kV} (1.2 \text{ kV})$
    * *Standard Rating:* $1.8/3 \text{ kV} (3.6 \text{ kV})$

---

## 2. Construction Requirements

### 2.1 Conductors
* Must be **Class 1** (solid) or **Class 2** (stranded) of plain/metal-coated copper or aluminum (per **IEC 60228**).
* *Note:* Class 5 (flexible) conductors are permitted for specific uses but are less common in fixed 60502-1 installations.

### 2.2 Insulation
The standard defines specific compounds and their maximum operating temperatures:

| Insulation Type | Compound | Abbreviation | Max Conductor Temp (Normal Operation) | Max Short Circuit Temp (5 sec) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Thermoplastic** | Polyvinyl Chloride | **PVC/A** | 70°C | 160°C (<300mm²) / 140°C (>300mm²) |
| **Thermosetting** | Cross-linked Polyethylene | **XLPE** (GP8) | 90°C | 250°C |
| **Thermosetting** | Ethylene Propylene Rubber | **EPR** (GP7) | 90°C | 250°C |

### 2.3 Identification (Core Colors)
While local regulations often supersede this, IEC 60502-1 references **IEC 60446** for identification:
* **Two-core:** Blue, Brown
* **Three-core:** Brown, Black, Grey
* **Four-core:** Blue, Brown, Black, Grey
* **Five-core:** Blue, Brown, Black, Grey, Black

### 2.4 Mechanical Protection (Armour)
If armour is required, it is placed over the inner covering (bedding).
* **SWA:** Galvanized Round Steel Wire (common for multi-core).
* **AWA:** Aluminum Wire Armour (required for **Single Core** cables to prevent hysteresis/eddy current heating).
* **STA:** Double Steel Tape (lighter duty).

### 2.5 Outer Sheath
* **PVC (Type ST1/ST2):** Common general purpose.
* **PE (Type ST3/ST7):** Polyethylene, harder, water-resistant.
* **Halogen Free (ST8):** Low smoke properties (LSZH).

## 4. Testing Summary

### 4.1 Routine Tests (100% of cables)
1.  **Electrical Resistance:** Conductor resistance must not exceed IEC 60228 max values.
2.  **Voltage Test:** 3.5 kV AC for 5 minutes (for 0.6/1 kV cables). No breakdown allowed.

### 4.2 Sample Tests
* **Check of dimensions:** Insulation/sheath thickness.
* **Hot set test (XLPE/EPR only):** Sample loaded at 200°C to ensure cross-linking is sufficient.

### 4.3 Type Tests
* **Electrical:** High voltage duration tests (4h).
* **Non-Electrical:** Aging in air oven, water absorption, fire retardancy (IEC 60332), gas emission.
